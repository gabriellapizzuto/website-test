About Our Group

The **AARCH Group** at the University of Liverpool is dedicated to cutting-edge research in architecture, focusing on sustainable design, digital fabrication, urban resilience, and historical preservation. Our interdisciplinary team comprises architects, engineers, historians, and computer scientists, all committed to pushing the boundaries of architectural knowledge and practice.

We engage in both theoretical and applied research, collaborating with industry partners, local communities, and international institutions to address complex challenges facing the built environment. Our work aims to contribute to a more sustainable, equitable, and aesthetically rich future.

Our Research Areas

Sustainable Architecture

Investigating eco-friendly materials, energy-efficient building systems, and passive design strategies to minimize environmental impact.

Digital Fabrication & Robotics

Exploring advanced manufacturing techniques, robotic construction, and computational design tools for complex geometries.

Urban Resilience & Planning

Developing strategies for climate-resilient cities, smart urban infrastructure, and community-led planning initiatives.

Architectural History & Theory

Analyzing historical architectural movements, theoretical frameworks, and their relevance to contemporary practice.

Human-Computer Interaction in Architecture

Designing intuitive interfaces and immersive experiences for architects and users in virtual and augmented reality environments.

Building Performance Simulation

Utilizing advanced simulation tools to optimize building energy performance, daylighting, and thermal comfort.
